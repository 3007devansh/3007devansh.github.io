## Read this before filling anything

### 1. We are a NEW college. We have no track record. That is FINE — but we must not fake one.

DEKSONS is in its **first-ever admission cycle**. That means the following simply **do not exist yet**, and we will **NOT** put them on the website:

| Do NOT collect / publish | Why |
|---|---|
| Placement percentage, "highest package", "average package" | We have placed zero students. Publishing numbers would be false advertising. |
| Alumni testimonials, student testimonials | We have no alumni and no completed students. |
| "Students placed" counts, recruiter testimonials | Same. |
| Awards, achievements, convocation photos | None yet. |
| Past exam results / pass percentage | No batch has appeared for exams. |

**Inventing any of these is a legal and reputational risk.** Parents in Akola talk to each other. One discovered false claim ends the institute's reputation permanently.

### 2. Instead, we build trust with what a new institute legitimately HAS.

This is the **Trust Substitution Strategy**. See Section B of the College Questionnaire. In priority order:

1. **NCVT / DGT affiliation certificate + Institute Code + MIS Code** — the single strongest trust signal. Parents can verify this themselves on `ncvtmis.gov.in`.
2. **The Gujarati Samaj's own history** — how long has Shri Akola Gujrati Samaj existed? What else does it run (schools, trusts, hospitals, charities)? This is *borrowed credibility*, and it is completely legitimate. A 50-year-old samaj starting a college is a very different story from a stranger starting one.
3. **Brand-new infrastructure** — we have excellent photos of a clean, newly built campus. New is an ASSET here. Lead with it.
4. **Faculty qualifications and prior experience** — where did they teach/work before?
5. **Industry MoUs, apprenticeship tie-ups, letters of intent** — even a signed intent letter is real and publishable.

### 3. How Maharashtra ITI admission actually works — this defines the website.

⚠️ **MANAGEMENT MUST CONFIRM THIS.** Maharashtra ITI admissions are centralized through the DVET portal (`admission.dvet.gov.in`). Students fill an **option form** and choose institutes by **Institute Code**.

If that applies to us, then:

> **The website does not take admissions. It drives students to enter our Institute Code in their DVET option form, and captures enquiries so we can call them.**

The most important thing on the entire website becomes the **Institute Code**, displayed large, with step-by-step instructions on how to select DEKSONS in the portal.

**Question for management:** Are we (a) admitting through the centralized DVET portal, (b) a private unaided ITI doing our own admissions, or (c) both (some seats each)? **The answer changes the entire site design.** Answer this first.

## Two important strategy notes

### Language: the site must speak Marathi, not only English
Akola is a Marathi-speaking district. The **students and parents** we are recruiting read Marathi. The founding body is Gujarati, but the audience is not.

**Recommendation:** English + Marathi. Marathi is not optional for parent trust. Gujarati is unnecessary for the public site.
**Question:** Who on staff can write/verify Marathi copy?

### Branding: do not lead with "Gujarati Samaj" in SEO
A student searching for a course types **"ITI Akola"**, **"fitter trade Akola"**, **"NCVT ITI near me"**. Nobody searches "Gujarati Samaj ITI".

**Recommendation:** "Gujarati Samaj" belongs in the *About / Trust* section, where it builds credibility as the founding body. It should **not** dominate the homepage, page titles, or SEO keywords — that narrows our audience and can read as exclusionary to the local Marathi majority we are trying to enrol.

**Question for management:** Do you agree the institute should be publicly positioned as **"DEKSONS Institute — an ITI in Akola"**, founded by Shri Akola Gujrati Samaj, rather than as a community institute?
