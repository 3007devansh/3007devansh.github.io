Getting your 10th result — good marks, low marks, or a subject not cleared — can
feel like a big moment. It is. But it is **not** the end of your options. In
Akola, there are real, practical paths to a good livelihood. Here is an honest
look.

## If you passed 10th

You can continue to 11th–12th, but that is not the only good choice. A **1–2 year
ITI course** teaches you a real skill and gets you working sooner — as an
electrician, computer operator, solar technician and more. Many students earn
while their friends are still studying, and can still study further later through
apprenticeship or a diploma.

## If your marks were low

Marks do not decide your future — **skills do.** Employers hiring an electrician
or welder care whether you can do the job, not your 10th percentage. An ITI
certificate is a nationally recognised (NCVT) qualification that opens factory
jobs, apprenticeships, and even government-job routes.

## If you did not clear 10th

You still have a strong option: the **Welder course is open after 8th standard.**
If school did not work out, welding is a genuine path to a skilled trade, a
steady income, and eventually your own workshop. Do not let one result stop you.

## Why an ITI is a smart choice

- **Short and practical** — 1 to 2 years, mostly hands-on, not just theory.
- **Job-focused** — every course leads to specific, in-demand work.
- **Recognised everywhere** — an NCVT certificate is valid across India.
- **Affordable** — fees are as per Government norms, and scholarships/freeships
  are available for SC/ST/OBC and minority students.
- **A foundation to build on** — apprenticeship, diploma lateral entry, or your
  own business are all open afterwards.
